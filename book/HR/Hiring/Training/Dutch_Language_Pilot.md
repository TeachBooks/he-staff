# Dutch Language Pilot

The Dutch Language Pilot is a two-year programme for academic staff in Hydraulic Engineering and 3ME who would like to develop their Dutch language skills in a flexible and structured way. The pilot supports colleagues in using Dutch more confidently in teaching, collaboration with Dutch-speaking stakeholders, university roles and daily life in the Netherlands.

The programme consists of one-to-one Dutch lessons tailored to your current level, learning goals and availability. The learning pathway is designed to support progression towards at least B2 level, with the possibility to continue towards C1 where relevant.


## What is it?

The Dutch Language Pilot offers individual Dutch language training for eligible academic staff in HE and 3ME. Lessons are organised in cycles of 12 one-to-one lessons and are planned between you and your teacher. The programme starts from your assessed Dutch level and includes progress reviews after each cycle. When you complete a study book, you may take a written and oral assessment and receive a certificate after successful completion of a language level.

Lessons are primarily offered online and are designed to fit academic schedules as much as possible. In addition to the individual lessons, the pilot may include complementary activities such as Dutch Grammar Bootcamp and Conversation Café sessions. These activities offer additional opportunities to practise Dutch in an interactive setting, with attention to grammar, everyday communication and, at higher levels, work-related interaction.

The pilot is intended for academic staff members with permanent contracts in HE and 3ME.


## What is expected of me?

Participation requires an active commitment to learning Dutch. The expected workload is approximately four hours per week: one hour of instruction and around three hours of self-study, homework and revision. Progress depends not only on attending lessons, but also on regular preparation and independent practice.

Participants are expected to attend lessons consistently, actively engage with the programme and sign a learning agreement outlining expectations around participation, workload and progression. Anonymised information about participation and progress may be used to evaluate the pilot. Individual progress data will be handled in accordance with applicable data protection regulations.

Before enrolling, please consider how the programme fits into your schedule and discuss your interest with your section head or head of department where relevant.


## How do I enroll?

Eligible staff members will be contacted about their interest in participating. Your section leader will be included in the process, and you are encouraged to discuss your participation with them.

If you indicate that you would like to participate, you will receive a link to an online registration form. In this form, you can indicate practical preferences such as preferred lesson times and start date. Kickstart will then match you with a teacher and schedule the lessons. Before starting, you will be asked to sign a learning agreement.

Starting later in the year is possible. Please still register when you already know you would like to participate, even if your preferred start date is later.
