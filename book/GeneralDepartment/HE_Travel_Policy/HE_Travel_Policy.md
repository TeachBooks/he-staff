# Travel Policy

On this page, you will find information about the general TU Delft travel policy and international work. 

## General policy

Are you planning to travel for work? If so, this is either an international or a domestic business trip. It is important - and mandatory - to seek approval from your line manager, also if you need an advance payment. 

All information about the general travel policy surrounding domestic and international business trips can be found on the following intranet pages:

-	[International business trip](https://intranet.tudelft.nl/human-resources-en/international-business-trip?p_l_back_url=%2Fnl%2Fgroup%2Fguest%2Fsearch%3Fq%3Dtravel%2Bpolicy&p_l_back_url_title=Search)
-	[Domestic business trip](https://intranet.tudelft.nl/human-resources-en/domestic-business-trips)


A few important points:

-	Consider if the international business trip really is necessary or whether alternatives (online or otherwise) are available.
-	All international business trips need to be booked via BCD travel; they have travel and accommodation options available.
-	Booking via other websites (for example when no accommodation is available, or the options on BCD are much more expensive) is allowed with permission from your line manager.
-	It is especially important to always try to book the flight via BCD for security reasons.


## International work

If, as a member of staff employed by TU Delft, you carry out work outside the Netherlands, this is known as cross-border employment. Within TU Delft we use the term ‘International Work’ (IW) for this. When working internationally, it is important to know in advance what this entails and what the consequences might be for you. You need explicit permission from your line manager to work internationally. Please note that working internationally is only considered ‘International work’ from 30 days onwards. 

All information regarding international work can be found on the following intranet page: 

-	 [International work](https://intranet.tudelft.nl/human-resources-en/working-internationally-1?p_l_back_url=%2Fnl%2Fgroup%2Fguest%2Fsearch%3Fq%3Dinternational%2Bwork&p_l_back_url_title=Search)

As explained on the intranet page, there are five key factors to consider when thinking about international work:

-	Social security
-	Wage tax
-	Applicable employment law
-	Pension
-	Immigration

Please read the page thoroughly before going to your line manager with questions about the process. If anything is still unclear, reach out to the HR advisor with any unanswered questions.
